Process

The basic breakdown of what will happen when we work together.

  • Stage 1

    Consultation

    Before our first complimentary consultation, I'll ask you to gather some examples of designs you love; this way, when we meet for coffee we'll be able to talk about your ideas and inspirations, as well as your needs, budget, and timeline.

  • Stage 2

    Design Proposal

    Within a week of our initial consultation, I'll email you a detailed project proposal, which will reflect the design direction of your project, as well as the timeline, pricing, and materials. As soon as the contract is signed and a deposit is made, the fun part begins!

  • Stage 3

    The Designing

    I'll take all of the information you give me and come up with a beautiful design. You'll have the opportunity to see your project at several points before completion, so we'll be able to fine-tune the details and make sure everything is perfect, and exactly as you imagined it.

  • Stage 4

    Delivery & Rejoicing

    After you give me your stamp of approval and I receive the remaining balance owing, your design will be sent to the printers or ready to launch online. Hurray!

Dreadful Art

Dreadful art for awful people.

Dreadful Art is a new art project I started at the beginning of 2015. I wanted to create a series of prints that satirize popular design trends, such as hand lettering, foiling, digital watercolour, "retro" effects, and type over the ubiquitous "nature photo". I will be updating the series periodically, and am selling the designs in various forms (multi-sized prints, canvas, shirts, totes, rugs, clocks, mugs, etc etc) online here.

  • Date: January 2015, onwards
  • Purchase: on Society6.com

Wedding Design

Various Clients

I was approached last year to create an invitation suite for two lovely clients, Tiffany and Michael. They're having a summer wedding, and wanted beautiful invitations to match their garden theme and colour scheme (lavender, buttercup, and sage). A few short chats and a visit to their beautiful community garden wedding venue later, we'd come up with the perfect design, which were printed on a beautiful, thick textured recycled cardstock. I couldn't have been happier with how they turned out!

This is what Tiffany had to say:

"We met with Rachel about a year before our wedding to discuss stationery. Michael and I had a bunch of highly unrelated thematic ideas about what we wanted our invitations to look like. Rachel took a look at our pinterest board, asked us some questions about our venue and decor, and helped us come up with a whimsical floral theme that was exactly what we had been imagining!

We knew we needed our invitations to include directions, because our ceremony location is tucked away and hard to find - even on Google Maps - and yet Rachel designed a map that makes the route appear simple (and somehow adorable)!

Also, apparently when Rachel went to pick up our Save the Dates, some of the cards had been double printed, making the writing wonky and the images unclear. However, we never would have even known, because before Rachel gave the cards to us, she went through every single invite, discovered the error, had the printer replace the troublesome cards, and we received a perfect set, none the wiser of the error.

Needless to say, we were thrilled with the invitations we received, and they turned out better than we ever could have pictured! They now figure prominently on the fridges of friends and family members. Almost every guest who has RSVP'd to the wedding has had a comment about how beautiful, cute, and uniquely 'us' our invitations are.

Rachel is incredibly awesome, and we are so grateful to her for helping us set the tone for our big day with her stationery!"


Pictures of the invitation suite are forthcoming.

  • Date: October 2014
  • Clients: Tiffany & Michael

Alex Hass Design + Art

Website Design and Development

Alex and I worked together to create a new portfolio website to showcase her beautiful images and design work.
